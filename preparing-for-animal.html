Preparing for an Animal


What do you need for a new animal?

  • High quality pet food. Be aware that some animals may be allergic to certain foods. If feeding a new food brand, monitor animal for the first week to ensure they do not have any type of allergic reactions to the food.

  • A bed. Although many animals would not mind sleeping on the ground, having a cot or bed for your animal will help them feel comfortable in a new environment.

  • Collar/harness and leash. Depending on how well your animal walks on the leash, you may want to consider a harness, collar, or both to help guide your animal to proper walking on the leash. Another additional option to have is a long leash to help with training.

  • Enrichment tools/toys. Animals get bored too! Don't forget to stock up on enrichment tools that can keep your animal mentally occupied. Did you know twenty minutes of mental stimulation is equivalent to a hour walk in terms of enrichment!

  • Patience. Remember these animals have been living in the shelter for a period of time and may take time to get accustomed to your home. Give them the time to adjust to the new environment and smells within your house.

  • Love. All animals just want a family to live their life out with. Provide these animals with the love they deserve.
